
# Risk Factors

Participation in Apollo Care involves risks.  This section summarises key risk categories to assist prospective members, token holders and contributors in making informed decisions.  It is not exhaustive and does not replace professional advice.

## Regulatory & Legal Risk

Apollo operates as a cost‑sharing program and is not licensed as an insurance company.  Changes in laws or enforcement priorities could classify Apollo as insurance and subject it to regulatory requirements.  Apollo intends to pursue licences in future phases, but there is no guarantee that authorisation will be granted or that regulatory regimes will remain favourable.

## Smart‑Contract Risk

Apollo relies on smart contracts on Solana for contributions, claims processing and token governance.  Bugs, vulnerabilities or exploits could result in loss of funds or unexpected behaviour.  While audits and community review mitigate risk, no smart contract is completely risk‑free.  Users should understand the technical risks of interacting with blockchain contracts.

## Market & Token Risk

$APH is a utility/governance token.  Its value may fluctuate due to market conditions, liquidity, demand and speculation.  The token has a fixed supply, but there is no guarantee of price appreciation or yield.  Rewards to members or stakers, if any, come from the Community & Ecosystem Fund and are not tied to premium payments or guaranteed.

## Governance Risk

Decisions are made by the DAO.  Poor governance choices (e.g., underfunding reserves, setting inappropriate ShockFactor levels) could harm members or token holders.  Token holder participation is important to maintain sound governance.  Concentration of tokens among a few holders could influence outcomes.

## Operational Risk

The protocol depends on oracles, AI services and external providers.  Failures or inaccuracies in these components may cause claim delays, incorrect pricing or security vulnerabilities.  Apollo aims to maintain redundancy and transparency but cannot eliminate all operational risk.  Provider networks and payment gateways are subject to third‑party performance and compliance.

## Liquidity & Solvency Risk

Claims volatility, pandemics or black swan events could exceed reserves.  Apollo mitigates this via its three‑tier model (liquidity buffer, operating reserve and contingent capital)and by purchasing reinsurance, but there is no guarantee that reserves will be sufficient.  In extreme cases, claims may be delayed, enrolment may be paused or contributions may be increased.

## Counterparty Risk

Reinsurers, custodians, payment processors and investment counterparties may default or become insolvent.  Apollo vets counterparties and uses diversified partners where possible, but counterparty risk remains.

## Technology & Adoption Risk

Apollo depends on the adoption of blockchain technology by members, providers and regulators.  If Solana or associated technologies face security issues, scalability challenges or loss of trust, Apollo’s operations could be impacted.  Additionally, slow adoption by providers or members could limit network effects.

By participating in Apollo Care, you acknowledge these risks and agree that neither the DAO nor contributors are liable for losses arising from these risks.  Always conduct your own research and consult professionals.
