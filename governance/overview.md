# Governance Overview

Apollo Care operates under a decentralised autonomous organisation (DAO) model.  Governance rights are attached to $APH tokens; holding $APH is optional for coverage members【14317910318221†L97-L110】, but those who do hold the token participate in shaping the protocol.

## Voting & Proposals

Token holders can submit proposals via the on‑chain governance contract.  Proposals may cover parameter changes (e.g., ShockFactor, contribution rates), election of committee members, capital allocation or changes to smart contract code.  A typical governance cycle includes:

1. **Proposal Drafting:** any $APH holder can draft a proposal.  Proposals must include a rationale, parameter changes and expected impacts.
2. **Committee Review:** relevant committees may provide recommendations or amendments before the proposal goes to a vote.
3. **Voting Period:** proposals are posted on‑chain with a start and end block/time.  Token holders vote “for,” “against” or “abstain,” weighted by their token balance (including any delegated tokens).
4. **Quorum & Approval:** proposals require a minimum participation (quorum).  Critical proposals (e.g., reserve drawdowns) may require a supermajority.  If passed, the proposal is executed automatically or queued with a time delay.

Delegation is supported: holders may delegate their voting power to trusted participants or committees without transferring ownership.  Vote delegation can be changed at any time.

## Committees

To manage day‑to‑day operations, the DAO elects committees of skilled members.  Committees have delegated authority within defined limits.  Major decisions and deviations require a full DAO vote.  Primary committees include:

* **Risk/Actuarial Committee:** sets contribution parameters, monitors loss ratios, adjusts ShockFactor within preset limits and recommends capital calls【214961381664349†L172-L195】.  Oversees actuarial modelling and reserves.
* **Claims Committee:** reviews escalated claims, determines payment amounts and handles appeals from members【214961381664349†L845-L899】.
* **Treasury Committee:** manages reserves, executes reinsurance contracts, invests surplus in low‑risk instruments and oversees Tier 2 activations.
* **Growth Committee:** directs marketing, partnerships, membership education and community incentives.
* **Compliance Committee (Future Phases):** once Apollo moves toward regulated insurance, this committee interfaces with regulators and ensures filings meet legal requirements【830535318348417†L180-L198】.

Committee members are elected by token holders for fixed terms.  They may be recalled by governance for misconduct or underperformance.

## Safeguards & Transparency

* **Delegated Limits:** each committee has limits on the actions it can take without a DAO vote (e.g., ShockFactor increases up to +20%, reserve drawdowns up to a threshold).  Exceeding these limits triggers an automatic governance proposal.
* **Transparency Reporting:** committees publish regular reports of metrics, decisions and expenditures.  The protocol maintains real‑time dashboards showing reserve levels, CAR, ShockFactor and pending claims【214961381664349†L1655-L1668】.
* **Appeals:** members can appeal claim decisions.  Appeals first go to the Claims Committee; if still unresolved, a DAO‑wide vote may be triggered for exceptional cases【214961381664349†L845-L899】.
* **Conflict of Interest Policies:** committee members must disclose conflicts and recuse themselves when appropriate.  Dual committee membership may be restricted.

## Governance Evolution

Apollo’s governance structure is designed to evolve.  As the protocol enters new phases (e.g., regulated insurance), governance may add compliance committees, adjust quorum thresholds or introduce new role‑based voting (e.g., provider representatives).  All such changes require DAO approval and will be communicated transparently.
