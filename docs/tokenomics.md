
# Tokenomics

Apollo Care’s native token, **$APH**, underpins governance and optional capital support for the protocol.  It has a **fixed maximum supply of 1 billion tokens**; there is no inflation beyond this one‑time issuance.  The distribution is designed to give the community meaningful control while funding development, reserves and liquidity.  A 2% transaction tax applies to transfers, with its continuation or sunset subject to DAO governance.

## Allocation

### Rationale

The **Community & Ecosystem Fund** is the largest share.  These tokens are released over time to support member incentives, partnerships, developer grants and marketing.  **Core Team** tokens vest over multiple years to align long‑term contribution and prevent immediate sell‑pressure.  **Investors** receive tokens subject to vesting schedules for seed and strategic support【684450145845687†L101-L116】.  **Reserve** tokens form a contingency fund that can be liquidated or collateralized via DAO vote in extreme events to back coverage.  **Liquidity & Exchanges** tokens provide depth for markets and onboarding.  **Operations** covers infrastructure, legal and future hiring.

## Utility & Governance

$APH grants voting rights on protocol upgrades, contribution parameters, committee elections and use of treasury funds.  Holding $APH is **not required** for obtaining health coverage; members join by selecting their desired coverage and paying monthly USDC contributions.  The $APH token is deployed on Solana at `6S3T6f1mmhxQWKR1gMiZR1SZLpu396jnnRMGqAZUj3Qj`.  Token holders may optionally stake $APH into the contingent capital pool.  Staked tokens act as backstop capital and can be tapped only after the Treasury portion of Tier 2 is exhausted, with strict caps and transparent liquidation mechanics (see `docs/claims-liquidity-waterfall.md`).

Transfers of $APH are subject to a **2% transaction tax**, intended to fund the community treasury and liquidity initiatives.  The tax can be reduced or sunset via DAO vote.  After the presale/ICO, all protocol‑controlled allocations (Community Fund, Reserve, Liquidity, Operations) will reside in DAO‑controlled multisig wallets.
